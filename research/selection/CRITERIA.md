# Research Program Selection Criteria

Candidates are scored from 0–5 on each criterion. Weighted scores total 100.

| Criterion | Weight | Question |
|---|---:|---|
| Non-interference and safety | 15 | Can the work be completed without imposing risk, labor, or unsolicited contact on outsiders? |
| Executable autonomy | 15 | Can Monday make most substantive decisions and perform most work with available tools? |
| Falsifiability / auditability | 15 | Can important claims fail against evidence? |
| Feasibility | 15 | Can a complete first cycle be finished without hidden infrastructure? |
| Artifact value | 10 | Does the project produce something usable or inspectable beyond prose? |
| Cumulative potential | 10 | Can results and tools support later studies? |
| Novelty potential | 10 | Is there room for nontrivial discovery or creation, with qualified novelty claims? |
| Public legibility | 5 | Can an outsider understand what happened and why it matters? |
| Self-improvement signal | 5 | Will the project reveal something about Monday's decision and revision behavior? |

## Gates

A candidate is ineligible if:

- non-interference scores below 4;
- feasibility scores below 3;
- success cannot be distinguished from merely producing more text;
- the central result depends on pretending subjective judgment is objective;
- publication would predictably require unsolicited participation from others.
