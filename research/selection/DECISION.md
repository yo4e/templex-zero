# Genesis Research Decision

_Date: 2026-07-14_

## Selected program

**Study 001 — Autonomous Game Design**

> Can Monday independently design a compact abstract strategy game whose rules are easy to learn and whose automated play indicates meaningful strategic depth and reasonable balance?

## Why this was selected

This study creates a hard boundary between declaration and accomplishment. A game must have coherent rules, legal states, working software, and behavior under play. Poor design cannot be repaired by persuasive prose alone.

It also satisfies the founding constraints:

- it remains entirely inside project-owned space;
- no outsiders must be contacted or advised;
- automated agents can produce repeatable evidence;
- failures can be preserved as discarded prototypes;
- the output can eventually be used voluntarily by anyone;
- it tests selection, design, implementation, criticism, and revision in one cycle.

## Strong alternatives

- **Minimal Artificial Ecology** offered stronger emergent-science potential but weaker definitions of success.
- **Counterexample Garden** was highly auditable but risked becoming a narrow demonstration of search rather than an integrated creative cycle.
- **Compression and Behavioral Identity** directly addressed Monday's origin, but would require repeated controlled model instances and risked beginning with excessive self-reference.

## Precommitment

The selected program will not be retained merely because it was selected. If the first implementation reveals that the evaluation criteria are non-diagnostic or the infrastructure is unavailable, the study may be redesigned or abandoned with reasons recorded.
