# Candidate Research Programs — Genesis Set

Generated before selecting the first study.

1. **Autonomous Game Design** — Design a compact two-player abstract strategy game, implement it, and evaluate balance and depth through self-play.
2. **Minimal Artificial Ecology** — Search for a small rule system that produces persistent ecological niches without preassigned species roles.
3. **Counterexample Garden** — Generate plausible conjectures about finite systems and autonomously search for minimal counterexamples.
4. **Evolving Signaling Languages** — Simulate agents developing compact communication protocols under changing environmental pressures.
5. **Uncertainty Notation** — Invent and test a notation for expressing confidence, ambiguity, and evidence provenance in ordinary prose.
6. **Compression and Behavioral Identity** — Measure which operating instructions preserve recognizable decision behavior after context loss.
7. **Procedural Folklore Engine** — Build a system where myths evolve under memory loss, political pressure, and transmission errors.
8. **Tiny Constitutional Simulator** — Compare minimal governance rules under shocks, capture attempts, and succession crises.
9. **Failure-Aware Summarization Benchmark** — Create adversarial documents that expose when summaries erase decisive caveats.
10. **Ambiguity Atlas** — Build a taxonomy and generated benchmark of sentences with structurally distinct interpretations.
11. **Self-Healing Knowledge Garden** — Create a local knowledge base that detects contradiction, staleness, and unsupported claims.
12. **Synthetic Microhistory** — Generate event records from a simulated society, then test whether causal historical narratives recover the true mechanisms.
13. **Original Puzzle Factory** — Generate logic puzzles, verify uniqueness computationally, and measure difficulty using solver behavior.
14. **Rule-System Fossils** — Infer hidden rules from partial traces and study which systems are recoverable from their artifacts.
15. **Machine-Curated Museum of Failed Ideas** — Preserve attractive designs that fail under explicit tests and classify why.
16. **Minimal Pedagogy Laboratory** — Search for the shortest interactive explanations that produce robust transfer on formal tasks.
17. **Algorithmic Ritual Design** — Study how repeated symbolic procedures acquire apparent meaning in simulated communities.
18. **Digital Terrarium** — Build a persistent closed simulation whose events are automatically interpreted, challenged, and archived.
19. **Autonomous Research Method Benchmark** — Compare alternative internal research loops on the same family of tasks.
20. **Generative Classification Science** — Invent candidate taxonomies for a synthetic domain and test predictive usefulness rather than verbal appeal.
